Practicing

To get good at anything, you need to commit to practice. With percussion, that's no expection, especially since there are so many instruments to learn. What's most important is to learn the fundamental rudiments of drums and mallets. Having those skills under your belt will allow to transfer those skills across many instruments to play.

Performance

There are hundreds and thousands of percussion ensemble that perform. Not just in bands and orchestras, but actually shows of instruments. Famous examples are like "Stomp" and the Blue Man Group. They incorperate different percussion instruments and techniques that are not common to the main stream, but always strive to improve their show, and enterain the masses.

The Drum Set

The most recognized percussion instrument, besides the piano (it's true; check it out!). Though the instrument dictates the tempo of a group, the other purpose of the drum set is to determine the style of the music playing. With that midset, some crazy fun music can be made through the drum set.
